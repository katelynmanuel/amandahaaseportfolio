The Truth About the Juice: Deep Diving the Celery Juice Trend


Move over kale and matcha, there's a new green drink in town and it's taking the wellness world by storm. But does this all-the-rage juice really live up to its hype? We're looking past the hashtags and diving into the science behind the celery trend.

What People are Saying

It is said that to experience optimal benefits, the stalk should be juiced without pulp and drank first thing in the morning on an empty stomach 30 minutes before eating anything.  The Medical Medium  claims that this morning elixir, when consumed over time, can provide benefits such as improved digestion, defense against pathogens, detoxification of the liver and other organs, and offers healing anti-inflammatory effects to the body. Yes, please!

What We Know

Celery is a nutrient-rich vegetable that is high in vitamins C and K, flavonoids, as well as potassium and folate to help reduce inflammation and boost immunity. It is also comprised of 95% water, offering hydrating and healing benefits to the liver and cardiovascular system. However, there is very little scientific research on the vegetable proving the wide variety of benefits the internet seems to claim.

Here's the Deal

We always love when health and wellness make it as a mainstream trend as long as the habit is actually nourishing. And celery juice is just that! Regardless of the lack of money and research spent so far on this green juice craze, the benefits it is providing to our bodies is promoting healthy habits and lifestyles which is a great thing. Filling your body with vitamins and hydration on an empty stomach can deliver the long-term benefits we're seeing today such as increased energy, clearer skin, improved digestion, and more.
